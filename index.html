Personal Statement

I grew up always worrying about my identity and wondering what lay at the core of it. Everyone around me had such dynamic personalities, bits and parts of which I adapted to be my own; over time, I began to feel a bit lost. Who was I truly, and what were my values? It wasn't until I began to consciously put in the effort to sort this out did I realize, I find my voice and stability at the intersection of various sources of input. I am the resilience of my family, the zest of my eccentric friends, and the affirmation of great mentors. I am host to the boldness of my father's mind and the simplicity of my mother's heart. It's at this intersection that I have been utterly convinced by close ones and strangers alike to live with purpose and that growth will always be a two way street, maybe three, or even four.
